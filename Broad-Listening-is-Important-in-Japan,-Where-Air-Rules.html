  • What is broad listening?

  • Advances in statistics can enhance democratic careful deliberation on a large scale, allowing “broad listening” where millions of people can hear the essence extracted from the distribution of opinions of their peers.

Broad listening methods that have been successful overseas will not work if brought directly to Japan.

  • Because the constraints of culture are different.
  • Broad listening is particularly suited to Japan’s consensus and hierarchical culture through its encouragement of the sharing of individual opinions and perspectives and their contribution to overall decision-making. However, Japan’s tendency toward “conflict avoidance” can make broad listening difficult to implement. Members are hesitant to express their views, preventing the open expression of all viewpoints and opinions that broad listening requires.
