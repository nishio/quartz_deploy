** Pen drying

A whiteboard marker written on a polypropylene whiteboard sheet with ordinary alcohol-based ink will dry out after a day and night in our environment and will not disappear whether rubbed with an eraser or by hand.

I thought I would have to throw it away because it did not disappear even after I sprayed it with water and blew on it with a wet wipe, but upon closer inspection, I realized that the wet wipe I used at the time was one that did not contain alcohol, so I wiped it with a wet wipe that did contain alcohol, and it easily cleaned up.

The "wet wipes containing alcohol" used at that time was this one from the company: <a href="http://www.amazon.co.jp/gp/product/B000FQNUGA/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=247& creative=7399&creativeASIN=B000FQNUGA&linkCode=as2&tag=nishiohirokaz-22">Elier sterilizable alcohol towels, 100 sheets per body</a>.

If you think about it, it might be useful to have a line on the whiteboard that you can rub off, and if you want to erase it, you can erase it with alcohol wipes.
