>>

No, you can just go to the citee's site and write "Thank you for the introduction" when the unauthorized citation is discovered. The person who reprinted the article should not reprint it without permission. The person who reprinted it should not reprint it without permission, but just ask "Can I reprint it?

It's one thing to have rights, it's another to exercise them. If the law is such that the right is not granted in this situation, it would be impossible to say to remove it even if the photo is quoted in the same way and the comment is "Here's the woman who took the picture in the mirror in the corner of this picture with her spunky face HAHAHA".

I don't think copyright law is a perfect fit for the current situation, but I think the current copyright law is the right one, at least in this case.
