Theanine (L-Theanine) is a derivative of glutamic acid, an amino acid found in large amounts in tea.

Theanine has been shown to have a variety of effects in clinical trials.

Theanine intake has been shown to produce alpha waves, an indicator of relaxation, 30-40 minutes after ingestion; 50 mg of theanine has been shown to relax individuals with low anxiety tendencies, and 200 mg of theanine has been shown to relax individuals with high anxiety tendencies (Kobayashi et al. 1998).

The same effect on anti-stress has also been confirmed. When stress was applied to people in a Kraepelin-type mental arithmetic task, and the effects of stress on heart rate, salivary immunoglobulin A, and subjective feelings of stress were examined, it was found that theanine intake suppressed stress (Kimura et al . , 2006). , 2006).
