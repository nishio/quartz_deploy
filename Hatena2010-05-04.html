*1272986799*Singapore Summary
- Singapore is roughly the same size as a ward of Tokyo.
- Income tax is capped at 20% and local taxes are non-existent. The effective tax rate for someone with an income of about 10 million is less than 10%.
- The maximum corporate tax rate is 18%. No cap on entertainment expenses. Tuition and school fees for family members of employees can also be expensed as part of the employee benefit package.
- No inheritance tax (which I think we should have...)
- There is no gift tax and no tax on gains on stock transfers. There is also no taxation on dividends or interest.
- An "Entrepreneur Visa" exists. Business is a resource that Singapore, with its small land area, can have in abundance, so it is actively trying to attract them.
- Private ownership of land is not allowed, so city planning by the state is very easy. It's smart. When developing underground, cities can be designed from scratch on a "new layer" because there is no need to bend over like in Japan. It is wonderful. In a few hundred years, we will have super-efficient underground cities like in science fiction. Less earthquakes.
- The image of "littering is punishable by a fine" gave the impression that police officers with batons would be watching on street corners, but this is not true. Compared to Japan, there are enough trash cans and ashtrays to deter people from littering by flashing a high fine. That's smart. I hardly saw any policemen.
- The services provided by local labor are generally inexpensive, probably because there is no minimum wage regulation. 200 yen will get you a fairly decent meal, and the first ride in a cab is 210 yen. 1,200 yen for a 25-kilometer ride. I heard that the monthly rate for hiring a maid is 20,000 yen. On the other hand, things imported from developed countries, such as Western books, Uniqlo, and iPads, are not cheap.
- The salary of a 40-year-old official is as high as 30 million yen, and a public official who receives a bribe application is illegal unless the donor is arrested. The risk of bribery applications has jumped and is a strong deterrent. That's smart. Furthermore, public officials are required to prove their integrity if they are suspected of bribery (although it may be better to punish those who are suspected of bribery), which is a deterrent to holding meetings where bribery is possible.
- Escalators are fast. No one wants to walk further on the escalator because it is faster than walking up the stairs. Naturally, the volume of traffic is doubled because one side of the elevator does not need to be opened. Smart.
- Some universities have six-month to one-year internships for credit. Proper grades are given.
- Convenience stores are full of 7-Eleven. Beverages are all over Pokka. Stationery stores are full of Kokuyo products.
- Trains are standardized to be Suica-like. Train fare is 140 yen if you ride from one end to the other. The maximum amount is deducted at the time of entry and returned at the time of exit, so there is no need for a recharging machine at the ticket gate. It's smart.
- Cars are taxed at higher rates to ease congestion, and the right to own a car is auctioned off. Smart.
- There is no discrimination based on skin color or race (including preferential legislation for Malays, as neighboring Malaysia does), but there is a wide disparity and poor service for low-income people. There are private cars for executives, cabs for us, buses packed with dark-skinned people, and trucks with 30 dark-skinned people in the back, though I don't know how many because I didn't get in. It is not discrimination based on race, so even Indians can have their own cars if their income is high enough.
- Pornography is illegal, but prostitution is legal only in government-run stores.
- There are surveillance cameras everywhere. Even on trains.
- All personal tax payments are online. The census is also online. It seems that the government offices are thinking, "Let's simplify and digitize the cumbersome paperwork, because we don't want to have to spend a lot of manpower on it. This is wonderful.
- National health insurance does not exist. Private insurance is available. It is common for companies to insure their employees as part of their benefits package.
