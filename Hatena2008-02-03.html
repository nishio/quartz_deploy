*1202042052* Writing camp

The first day goes well. The second day you are too tired to work. The same can be said for development camps, but you need an unwritten rule: don't play. We push ourselves. There should be a separate meeting for friendship and a meeting to focus on work. This time it worked out well, but both Python Hot Springs and the Unexplored Youth Development Camp fell into the easy way because the distinction between the two was not clear.

*1202044633* "Push Yourself" meeting proposal
What about calling it a "writing camp" when it does not involve overnight stays? Is it necessary to limit it to writing? I thought about it and came up with the name "Push Yourself to the Limit". What I write here is just an idea.

- It is not a social gathering, but rather a meeting to make progress in a situation where everyone around you is working silently. It is not a meeting for socializing, but a meeting to push yourself into a situation where "everyone around you is working silently" and try to make some progress in your slow work.
- Present a roadmap of what you will do and how far you will go on that day in advance. Since it takes time to make each presentation orally, each participant writes it on his/her own Lingr.
- If you are seen opening mixi or LDR or something, is that a fine?
- Everyone <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/4476">LeechBlock :: Firefox Add-ons</a> installed
- First, decide on an appropriate timetable, such as 90 minutes of work and 10 minutes of rest, etc., in order to create a deadline effect in small increments.
- Chatting is prohibited except during recess.

And so on. We can have as few participants as we want, and it would be easy to hold the event if we wanted to. At any rate, we would like to do it again next week.
