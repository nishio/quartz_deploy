It is not because of a weak will that people make plans and fail to realize them. It is because they make reckless plans that can never be realized, and then they fall further and further behind schedule and give up when they can no longer accomplish the task no matter what they think. So, I think we should first set up a period of time to start the process, and then quantitatively measure how many tasks can be done in how much time. Indeed, looking back on my past experiences of failure, I realized that I had made plans without measuring how much I could accomplish.

The other interesting classification is that there are some types of tasks that can be done in small chunks of time and some that can't. It's true that I can't do coding in 15 minutes. I used to play Othello (Morocco) on my iPhone when I had some free time on the train, but I was getting tired of it and thought that I should use this time to gather information or study.
