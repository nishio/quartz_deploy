*1318380596* Typical of study group problems.
<a href='http://d.hatena.ne.jp/hyoshiok/20111010#p1'>The Kernel Reading Group - Diary of sometime in the future/hyoshiok</a>. As Mr. Yoshioka wrote, the decline in frequency of study groups due to the high cost of procuring venues is "a typical example of a study group problem". 1000speakers, which I used to do with id:amachang, started out in a lab, but when it became impossible for various reasons, it suffered and stopped. I was not able to do it at the beginning.

However, I think my perception of "what is the essence of the problem" has changed somewhat since those days. So I will record it here.

Securing a venue is inherently costly, and planning how to procure one is part of the management's job. However, we were fortunate that the company was able to supply us with a low-cost venue: "All we had to do was hold down the dates and times available in the company's meeting room reservation system and inform the general manager that we would be holding a kernel reading session on what day. The company cost the company tens to hundreds of thousands of dollars for land, electricity, and air conditioning. The company provided this free of charge.

The situation has changed. They still provide us with everything at no cost, but the procedure has become slightly more cumbersome. The cost of dealing with delays has jumped.

Have you made it an objective to maintain all the "achievements that were once possible under the former circumstances" despite the fact that circumstances have changed? It doesn't say specifically, but if "there is also the cost of assigning receptionists, elevator attendants, etc. to handle late arrivals," but two people are held for an hour, then if the hourly wage is 2,000 yen, the cost is 4,000 yen, right? Should we pay that cost and still maintain the "let in late" response? Is that important to the "Kernel Reading Group"? Is it something you want to gain?

The uncritical "status quo" of "things that used to be easy to do but are now costing more," along with many other things, appears to be the cause of the high costs. Shouldn't we reevaluate what it was that we wanted to gain and discard those things that give little return for the cost?

I think most of the problem can be solved if we don't free-ride on the company to procure the venue, but pay for it ourselves and outsource it to a specialized company. I looked into a rental conference room that can accommodate 80 people, and paying 1000-2000 yen per person for a 4-hour rental would be sufficient. The reason why you cannot take that option is probably because the ideal that Mr. Yoshioka wants to protect is "no participation fee. That ideal has not been verbalized, and it is hard to read from that sentence. That's why people are saying, "Why don't you just charge for it?

Why not start with a verbal list of what the Kernel Reading Group is currently OR used to achieve? Things like "no entry fee," "10 meetings a year," "late arrivals accepted," "beer bash," etc. Then you would have to admit that you can't keep all of these and scrap some of them.
