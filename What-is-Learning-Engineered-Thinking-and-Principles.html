Reduce experimental validation cycles

Many people may feel that the process of hypothesis, experiment, and verification is a big deal. The key is how to make the cycle of experimental verification smaller and faster.

This concept became famous in the field of software engineering when it was introduced in the book ‚ÄúLean Startup. This book is about management strategies for software ventures. Software ventures develop software while consuming limited funds, and if they do not generate income before the funds run out, they will go bankrupt.

A software venture has a hypothesis: ‚ÄúIf we make this kind of software, it will sell. If this hypothesis is correct and they use all of their capital to develop the software, they will go bankrupt immediately if the hypothesis is wrong. The experiment of ‚Äúactually developing software and seeing if it sells‚Äù is too big. So we think about how we can make the experiment smaller. For example, Dropbox, a file-sharing service, made a video showing what the software could do before actually developing it.
